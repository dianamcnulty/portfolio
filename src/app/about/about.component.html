About Me

I consider myself a modern day renaissance woman. Life has brought my on so many journeys and all of them are connected.
My first experiences in the world of design were born from necessity, back in college when I was a broke music student trying to advertise my recitals without spending any of the money that I needed for textbooks and food. I had a computer, and a black, white printer, and an imagination. That was all I needed.
Later on when the world photography moved to the digital realm, I was able to incorporate my shutterbug habbit into my designs as well. Then eventually, I wanted a website to share my musical projects with the world... then my collegues wanted one too. I learned enough html and css to get my designs looking ok, but I wanted more!
Soon the opportunity of a lifetime would land in my inbox.
My employer (I have a day job that has nothing to do with music) had started a program to send folks like me, who wanted to learn to code, through a 3 month immersive program to become developers. It was incredible. I could never have afforded to do this on my own, so I studied hard, and applied. The moment I heard that I was accepted, I knew I had just stepped into a new glorious adventure, and I was eager.
I went to Boston for 3 months for the Web Development Immersive course at General Assembly. It was challenging but engaging. Despite how hard I worked, I had an incredible experience, and learned so much in 12 short weeks! That education serves as the springboard for the rest of my career as a developer as I continue to learn, experience, and experiment with new technologies and ideas.
